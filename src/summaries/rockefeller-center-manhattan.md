Rockefeller Center is a landmark complex of 19 buildings spanning 22 acres in Midtown Manhattan between 48th and 51st Streets, commissioned by John D. Rockefeller Jr. and constructed in the 1930s as a masterwork of Art Deco architecture and urban design. A designated New York City landmark and National Historic Landmark, the complex is renowned for its iconic sunken plaza, the Radio City Music Hall, its vast collection of public art, and the annual lighting of the Rockefeller Center Christmas Tree.
