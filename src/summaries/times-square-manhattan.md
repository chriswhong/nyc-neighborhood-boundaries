Times Square is the dazzling commercial crossroads of Midtown Manhattan, formed by the intersection of Broadway and Seventh Avenue between 42nd and 47th Streets, and globally recognized for its towering digital billboards, Broadway theaters, and relentless 24-hour energy. Renamed in 1904 after The New York Times relocated its headquarters there, it draws an estimated 50 million visitors annually and is home to the iconic New Year's Eve ball drop, cementing its reputation as the self-styled "Crossroads of the World."
