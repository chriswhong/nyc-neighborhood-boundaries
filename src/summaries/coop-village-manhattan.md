Cooperative Village is a community of four housing cooperatives on the Lower East Side, centered on Grand Street south of the Williamsburg Bridge approach, comprising 4,500 apartments across twelve buildings. The cooperatives were built and sponsored by labor unions — the Amalgamated Clothing Workers of America and the ILGWU — beginning in the 1930s, embodying a utopian vision of worker-owned housing that shaped one of the most significant affordable housing experiments in New York City history.
