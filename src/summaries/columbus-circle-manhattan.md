Columbus Circle is the dramatic traffic roundabout at the southwest corner of Central Park where Broadway, Eighth Avenue, and 59th Street converge, marked at its center by a monument to Christopher Columbus that has stood since 1892 and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. It also serves as the official point from which all highway distances from New York City are measured, and the surrounding neighborhood has evolved into one of Midtown's most prominent commercial and cultural nodes, anchored by the Time Warner Center and the Museum of Arts and Design.
