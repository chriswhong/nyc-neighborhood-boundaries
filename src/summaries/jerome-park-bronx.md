Jerome Park takes its name from the prestigious thoroughbred racetrack that operated here from 1866 to 1894, which hosted the inaugural Belmont Stakes in 1867 and the first outdoor polo match in the United States in 1876. The western half of the grounds was converted into the Jerome Park Reservoir, while the eastern half became home to institutions including Lehman College and the Bronx High School of Science, making the area one of the Bronx's key educational centers.
